Detailed Study Guide

What is Rainwater Harvesting?

Rainwater harvesting (RWH) is the process of collecting, storing, and using precipitation that falls on rooftops and other surfaces. It is one of the most ancient and effective techniques for sustainable water management.

India receives an average of 1,170 mm of rainfall annually, yet most of it is lost as surface runoff or evaporation. Rooftop RWH captures this water before it is lost, providing both direct use (drinking, irrigation) and indirect groundwater recharge.

The technique is especially vital in:

  • Water-scarce and drought-prone regions (Rajasthan, Gujarat)
  • Urban areas with over-exploited groundwater tables
  • Villages lacking piped water infrastructure
  • Areas with high fluoride / arsenic in groundwater

Types of Rooftop RWH Systems

1. Direct Use System — Rainwater is collected and stored in an above-ground tank for immediate domestic use (washing, toilets, gardening). Simple, low-cost, requires filtration.

2. Underground Sump — Collected water is directed underground into a large cistern. Protects water from contamination and evaporation. Suitable for urban households.

3. Recharge Pit — A shallow pit filled with gravel/sand that allows harvested water to slowly percolate into the soil. Replenishes shallow aquifers.

4. Recharge Well / Bore Well — Collected water is channelled into an existing or dedicated bore well, recharging deep aquifers. Most effective for large-scale groundwater restoration.

5. Percolation Tank — A constructed pond or tank that stores surplus water, allowing natural percolation over weeks/months. Used in rural and agricultural settings.

The Harvesting Potential Formula

The fundamental equation to estimate annual rainwater harvesting potential:

V = A × R × C ÷ 1000
V = Volume (m³/year)  |  A = Rooftop Area (m²)  |  R = Annual Rainfall (mm)  |  C = Runoff Coefficient

Runoff Coefficients by Roof Type:

  • Metal / GI Sheet roof → C = 0.90
  • Concrete / RCC roof → C = 0.85
  • Tiled / Mangalore roof → C = 0.75
  • Asbestos sheet → C = 0.80
  • Thatched roof → C = 0.60

The coefficient accounts for losses due to evaporation, absorption by the roofing material, and splashing during heavy downpours. Always subtract 10–15% more for first-flush diversion.

Example Calculation

Given:

  • Rooftop Area: 150 m²
  • Annual Rainfall: 800 mm
  • Roof Type: Concrete (C = 0.85)
V = 150 × 800 × 0.85 ÷ 1000
V = 102 m³/year
= 102,000 litres/year = ~279 litres/day average

This volume exceeds the daily water requirement of a 5-member household (55 L/person/day × 5 = 275 L/day), demonstrating the power of even a modest rooftop system.

For storage tank sizing: design the tank to hold the dry-season peak demand, typically 30–60 days' requirement.

Artificial Ground Recharge

When harvested water exceeds storage capacity or direct use demand, it can be directed to artificial recharge structures that replenish groundwater.

Recharge Pit Method: A 1–2 m diameter pit, 2–3 m deep, filled with coarse gravel, sand, and charcoal layers. Inlet at top, perforated sides allow slow percolation. Cost: ₹5,000–15,000.

Recharge Well: Filtered rooftop runoff is directed into an existing bore well or a dedicated shaft well. Can recharge aquifers 30–100 m deep. Cost: ₹20,000–50,000.

Benefits:

  • Raises water table by 1–5 m in 2–3 monsoon seasons
  • Reduces land subsidence in urban areas
  • Dilutes contaminants in over-exploited aquifers
  • Reduces urban flooding by absorbing peak runoff

Government Policies & Schemes

🏛️

Jal Shakti Abhiyan

National campaign launched 2019 for water conservation. Mandates RWH in all govt buildings, schools, hospitals. Targets 256 water-stressed districts.

🏙️

Urban RWH Mandate

Cities like Chennai, Bengaluru, Delhi made RWH compulsory for buildings >300 m². Non-compliance attracts penalties. Applicable to new constructions and renovations.

💰

MNREGA Integration

Mahatma Gandhi NREGS funds construction of percolation ponds, farm bunds, and recharge pits in rural areas through wage employment.

🌾

PMKSY (Har Khet ko Pani)

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana provides subsidies for on-farm water storage, including rooftop collection tanks for agricultural use.

🔬

CGWB Guidelines

Central Ground Water Board provides technical standards for artificial recharge structures. Offers free site assessments and design support in notified areas.
